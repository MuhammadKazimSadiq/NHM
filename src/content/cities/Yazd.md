  Yazd is a UNESCO-listed desert city famed for its
  windcatchers, mudbrick buildings, and deep
  Zoroastrian heritage. It’s one of the oldest
  continuously inhabited cities in the world, where
  tradition lives on through its ancient qanats and fire
  temples. The city’s unique architecture is perfectly
  adapted to the desert climate, making it a symbol of
  resilience and ingenuity.
